Bo Ne

Description

Bo Ne, or Vietnamese Sizzling Steak and Eggs, is a common Vietnamese breakfast dish but can be enjoyed at any time during the day.
The name literally translates to "dodging beef" as in dodging the splatter of butter and oil while the steak is sizzling on the hot plate that it's served on.
Bo means "cow" and Ne means "to dodge".
Bo Ne typically consists of steak, eggs, SPAM (or xa xiu), with Vietnamese baguette, pate, bo (Vietnamese mayo), and some sort of salad on the side.

Ingredients

  • beef steak (tenderloin, top loin, or sirloin)
  • oyster sauce
  • fish sauce
  • soy sauce
  • brown sugar
  • garlic
  • sesame oil
  • unsalted butter
  • yellow onions
  • banh mi baguette
  • cilantro (for garnishing)
  • pickled vegetables
  • pate
  • bo or Vietnamese mayo

Instructions (One Serving)

  1. Marinate Steak

    Slice steak into thick slices (about 1/8") against the grain.
    In a medium bowl, mix oyster sauce, fish sauce, soy sauce, brown sugar, garlic, and garlic oil.
    Toss steak in marinade. Let marinate for 15 minutes or in the fridge overnight, covered.

  2. Saute Onions and Sear Steak in Butter

    Heat cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat.
    Add 1/2 tsp of butter to pan.
    Add 1/4 of onions to pan and cook until softened and lightly charred (~5 minutes).
    Add 1/4 of marinated steak to pan and cook for 5 minutes or until desired level of doneness.

  3. Add Eggs and Tomatoes

    Once steak is done, add 1/2 tsp of butter to pan.
    Crack an egg into the butter and cook to desired doneness.

  4. Add Garnishes

    Garnish with cilantro and or green onions.
    Spread pate and bo on baguette.

  5. Enjoy!
